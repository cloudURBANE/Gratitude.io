Actual page: “Hold-to-Tip + Smart Dock”

1) Hold-to-Tip (signature gesture)
	•	Press & hold anywhere on the amount area → a circular meter materializes under the thumb (glass ring with liquid gradient).
	•	Slide up/down while holding to fine-tune the amount (snaps per $1; haptic tick on Android).
	•	Release to lock amount.
	•	Double-tap = repeat last tip instantly. Triple-tap = last tip + $1.
	•	Fallbacks: preset chips ($3/$5/$10/$20) and a numeric input; if prefers-reduced-motion is on, we skip the fluid animation and just scale the number.

Why it’s good: totally one-handed, zero hunting for tiny buttons, memorable, and still accessible.

2) Smart Dock (payment picker that adapts)
	•	A bottom glass dock shows 3 large icons: the best method first (Cash App on Android, Apple Pay/Card on iOS, Venmo otherwise), then two alternates.
	•	Tap = deep link with prefilled amount (we already have the formats).
	•	If we detect IG/FB in-app browser, the dock changes to “Open in Safari/Chrome” + a QR of the deep link (one tap to copy).

3) Micro-impact bar (replaces cheesy copy)
	•	A thin impact bar under the amount reads:
“Covers 12% of their rent goal” → updates as you adjust the amount.
	•	We’ll keep it subtle, not preachy. (It’s a single line, tiny icons, no paragraphs.)

4) Return-to-Review (only after the hand-off)
	•	When the user comes back from the payment app (page visible again), we slide up two glass chips: Google and Yelp.
	•	One more chip: “Add to Wallet” (Apple/Google wallet pass) → next time it’s one-tap repeat from their wallet card.

5) Visual language (premium, not flashy)
	•	Liquid gradient (purple→teal) inside the hold ring that thickens with amount.
	•	Edge highlight on primary elements; soft inner glow on interactive glass.
	•	Payment-aware halo: tapping Venmo/Cash App/Card briefly tints the halo then fades back to brand gradient.
	•	No layout jumps; 160–220ms transitions; respect prefers-reduced-motion.

6) Accessibility
	•	All touch targets ≥44px; the hold region is huge (full card).
	•	Live announcements: “Amount set to $10”, “Link copied.”
	•	Full keyboard parity: arrow keys adjust, Enter confirms.

Demo/Showroom: “Studio Mode” (never been done like this)

This is a separate route that sells the experience and lets you test every edge case without real money.

1) Device simulator (top)
	•	Toggle chips: iOS / Android / In-app Browser.
	•	Amount presets to simulate: $3 / $5 / $10 / Custom.
	•	Switches: Reduced Motion, Low Bandwidth, NFC tap.

2) Live phone canvas (center)
	•	Shows the exact UI the customer sees.
	•	Tapping “Pay” doesn’t leave your site — it opens a mock overlay that tells you exactly which deep link would fire (e.g., https://cash.app/$rack/7) and whether it would handoff to app or show the Open in Safari guard.

3) Telemetry strip (right or bottom)
	•	Live events stream: HOLD_START → AMOUNT: 7 → METHOD: CASH_APP → HANDOFF_OK → RETURN → REVIEWS_SHOWN.
	•	This proves polish to buyers and helps you debug in seconds.

4) QR & NFC generator (panel)
	•	Type a handle + default amount; it spits out a short link + QR and instructions to burn it onto an NFC tag.
	•	Tap “Simulate tap” to jump the phone canvas to the right state.

5) “Director” overlays (educational, not salesy)
	•	Hover lines that explain why the dock reordered (platform signal), why the amount defaulted (time of day), and how fallbacks behave. Toggle off for clean view.
