Here’s a tight monetization plan that keeps the “experience-first” vibe, uses ads only where they won’t hurt tips, and gives a clear Pro path.

Model (simple + defensible)

Free (ad-supported)
	•	1 TipLink, default theme, basic deep links.
	•	Ads only after the payment handoff (on the “return” / “thanks” screen) and on the worker’s dashboard.
	•	“Powered by TipVault” badge.

Pro
	•	$4.99/mo or $35/yr.
	•	No ads anywhere.
	•	Custom handle/branding, NFC/QR generator pack, link shortener, venue/day insights, Wallet pass, team-split, export CSV, remove badge.

Add-on store (any tier)
	•	NFC tap card + 20 QR minis: $15 kit (your cost ~$6–8 → margin $7–9).
	•	Extra profiles ($2/mo each).
	•	Custom domain ($3/mo).

⸻

Where the money comes from

1) Subscriptions (primary)
	•	Target: 10–15% of active users convert to Pro.
	•	Free is great for lead-gen; Pro is the engine.

2) Ads (secondary, non-intrusive)
	•	Placement:
	•	Return screen only (after they come back from Venmo/Cash App/Card).
	•	Worker dashboard side panel.
	•	Never on the pre-tip page (protects conversion).
	•	Format: 1 small contextual native card (e.g., rideshare, food, banking).
	•	Frequency cap: max 1 impression per session per visitor.
	•	Fallback: your own “house ads” (QR kits/NFC, Pro upgrade) when no fill.

3) Kits & upsells (high margin, simple ops)
	•	QR/NFC kits, vinyl stickers, lanyards.

⸻

Experiential guardrails (so ads don’t ruin it)
	•	Tip flow stays 2 taps; ads appear only after money is sent (or dismissed if the user never returns).
	•	In-app browser detection: if handoff fails and visitor never leaves, hide ads—help them open Safari/Chrome instead.
	•	Respect prefers-reduced-motion and keep the return ad static.

⸻

Implementation (web app details, concise)

DB
	•	users(id, plan: "free"|"pro", created_at, …)
	•	profiles(user_id, handle, payments…)
	•	impressions(id, handle, slot, ad_id, ts, ip_hash)
	•	purchases(user_id, sku, amount, ts)

Feature flags
	•	isPro = user.plan === "pro"
	•	canShowAds = !isPro

Ad decision
	•	Server-side function chooseAd({slot:"return", city, device, time})
	•	Prioritize house offers → affiliate/contextual network → none.
	•	Return {title, body, cta, url, trackingPixel?}.

UI slots
	•	<AdSlot slot="return" /> on /success only.
	•	<AdSlot slot="dashboard_side" /> in worker UI.

Privacy
	•	No third-party scripts on the tip page; render ad as static HTML from your server.
	•	If you later add personalized ads, show a tiny consent toggle on the return screen; default to contextual.

Pro gating
	•	Middleware checks plan; hides <AdSlot> and badge for Pro.
	•	Upgrade flow: Stripe Checkout (monthly/yearly) + proration disabled.

House ad examples
	•	“Make this page ad-free with TipVault Pro.”
	•	“Get your NFC tap card kit (tap to open payment with $ prefilled).”

⸻

Quick, realistic math (so you know what to expect)
	•	Ads: contextual CPM ~$1–$4. If a worker’s page gets ~1,500 return views/month → ~$1.5–$6 gross/month. (Good for you at scale, not for an individual user.)
	•	Subs: 100 paying users × $35/yr ≈ $3,500/yr; 500 → $17,500/yr (before Stripe fees).
	•	Kits: 100 kits/month × ~$8 margin ≈ $800/mo.

Conclusion: Subs + kits drive profit; ads are gravy and should never touch the pre-tip experience.
