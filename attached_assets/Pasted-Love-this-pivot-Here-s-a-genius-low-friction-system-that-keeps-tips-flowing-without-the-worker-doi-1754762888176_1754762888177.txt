TipLink Express (set-and-forget flow)

1) One-Tap Repeat (remembers the customer)

Idea: If a customer has tipped you before on that device, show a giant “Tip again: $X” button as the very first thing (plus a tiny “change amount” link).
Why it works: repeat behavior > persuasion. It’s familiar, fast, and respectful.

How to implement
	•	On first tip, store {amount, method} in localStorage.
	•	On load, if found, render One-Tap Repeat at the top and auto-prime the correct deep link.

// pseudo in /u/[handle]
const k = `tiplink:${handle}:last`;
const last = JSON.parse(localStorage.getItem(k) || "null"); // {amount: 10, method: "cashapp"}
if (last) showRepeat(last);
function saveLast(a:number,m:string){ localStorage.setItem(k, JSON.stringify({amount:a, method:m})) }

2) Smart Default (context-aware)

Idea: If it’s their first time, don’t show random presets. Use a smart default tuned by context:
	•	$5 weekday mornings, $7 Fri/Sat nights.
	•	If the bill/note amount is known (e.g., you typed it in), set 15–20%.
	•	If the device is iOS and Apple Pay is available, show Card/Apple Pay first; Android → Cash App first.

How to implement
	•	Lightweight rules engine: (day, hour, device, venueType?) → defaultAmount + primaryMethod.
	•	Preselect the dial to that amount and highlight the best method button.

3) NFC Tap Card (no camera, no QR)

Idea: Worker carries a tiny NFC card (credit-card or keyfob). Customer taps → phone opens your deep link with amount prefilled (e.g., cash.app/$rack/7 or Venmo universal URL).
Why it works: zero camera friction; magical feeling; perfect for repeat interactions.

How to implement
	•	Encode a short link like https://t.lnk/rack?a=7&m=cashapp.
	•	Your page instantly redirects to the native app deep link; if blocked (in-app browser), show “Open in Safari/Chrome” + QR fallback.

4) Shift QR (ephemeral, auto-rotating)

Idea: Each shift generates a fresh QR with a lightweight token (valid for, say, 8 hours). That token remembers the worker’s preferred method for that shift and the most effective default amount from past data.

Why it works: keeps analytics clean, resists old screenshots, and lets the system continuously self-optimize defaults without the worker doing anything.

How to implement
	•	/u/[handle]?t=<shiftToken> → server (or edge function) looks up {primaryMethod, defaultAmount}.
	•	Store conversions against shiftToken for learning; next shift’s defaults adapt.

5) Whisper Boost (polite, non-pushy nudge)

Idea: After they pick an amount, show a tiny “+ $1 keeps this worker ad-free” toggle (off by default). It’s respectful, visible, and not spammy.

How to implement
	•	One checkbox before opening the deep link. If on, bump the amount client-side before building the URL.

6) Wallet Pass (loyalty without accounts)

Idea: On success page, offer “Add TipLink to Wallet” (Apple/Google). The pass shows your avatar + a dynamic QR. Next time they open it from Wallet at your venue, tapping the QR jumps straight to One-Tap Repeat.

Why it works: zero login, zero app install; turns occasional tippers into regulars.

How to implement
	•	Generate an Apple Wallet .pkpass and a Google Wallet pass with your short link. Update the pass background seasonally; keep the QR static to your short link.

7) Return-Flow Reviews (only when they come back)

Idea: Detect when the user returns from Venmo/Cash App (page becomes visible again) and slide in two chips: “⭐️ Google” and “Yelp”. No reviews nag before they tip.

How to implement

document.addEventListener('visibilitychange', () => {
  if (document.visibilityState === 'visible') showReviewChips();
});

8) Team-Split (optional, 1 extra tap)

Idea: If the service was shared (barback + bartender), show “Split with team (50/50)” right under the amount. Selecting it reveals second person’s handle and builds two deep links—the UI opens the first, then shows a “Now tip 2/2” bar.

Why it works: customers often want to be fair; this makes it effortless.

How this stays “non-repetitive” for the worker

	•	One-Tap Repeat handles regulars automatically.
	•	NFC tap handles folks who don’t like QR.
	•	Wallet pass handles long-term loyalty without DMs or accounts.
